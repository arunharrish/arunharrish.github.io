Profile · Background · Skills

I'm a graduate student at Georgia Institute of Technology pursuing my MS in Mechanical Engineering, maintaining a perfect 4.0 GPA. My work sits at the intersection of high-fidelity simulation, machine learning, and design optimization.

From conjugate heat transfer on rotary engine cooling systems at LiquidPiston, to aerodynamic wind load prediction for tower cranes using parametric RANS CFD — I build models that are both accurate and actionable.

I'm particularly drawn to problems where surrogate modeling and DOE can replace expensive simulation loops, reducing computation time by 60%+ without sacrificing fidelity. My undergraduate background at PSG College of Technology (3.97/4.0) and three years leading a 25-member Baja SAE team ground everything in real engineering constraints.

Currently a Graduate Research Assistant at GT's Crane Safety Research Center, where I've also authored two accepted conference papers for ASME FEDSM 2026.
